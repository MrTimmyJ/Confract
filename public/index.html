FAQ

Common questions

Do I need to know which "mode" to use?
No. Confract automatically detects what your content is â€” a watchlist, research notes, code documentation, creative writing, anything. You paste it, Confract figures out the structure. You can override if needed, but most of the time you won't want to.
What's the "Consolidation Log" vs "duplicates"?
We deliberately don't call it a duplicate log. Confract detects overlapping content â€” semantically similar items, not just identical text. Everything it consolidates is logged with a reason, and you can restore any item with one tap. Nothing is silently deleted.
What's the API key and how do I get one?
An API key is like a password that lets Confract talk to Claude AI on your behalf. Here's how to get one in about 2 minutes:

1. Go to console.anthropic.com
2. Create a free account with your email
3. Click "API Keys" in the left menu â†’ "Create Key"
4. Copy the key (starts with sk-ant-api03-)
5. Paste it into Confract when prompted â€” that's it

Anthropic gives you free credits to start. Your key is saved only in your browser and goes directly to Anthropic â€” Confract never sees it. In the future, this step will be automatic and you'll just log in with email.
Can I lose data?
No. Every merge creates a version snapshot first. The Consolidation Log preserves every item that was removed or merged. Version History lets you revert to any previous state. Your knowledge base is append-only by design.
How is this different from Notion AI or NotebookLM?
Notion AI generates content and helps you write. NotebookLM answers questions about documents you upload. Confract does neither â€” it takes multiple messy inputs and converges them into one clean, deduplicated master document. It's a consolidation engine, not a writing assistant or a Q&A tool.
Where is my data stored?
Currently, all documents are saved in your browser's local storage. They're private by default and never sent to Confract's servers. Future versions will add optional cloud sync with full account-based storage.
